Sober since July 3rd, 2023 — one day at a time.

Hi, my name is Brian and I'm 33 years old.

For most of my 20s I struggled with alcoholism. I drank every day from morning to night, trying to escape severe anxiety and panic disorder. At the time, I convinced myself I just hadn't found the right medication yet — but in reality alcohol had become my way of avoiding everything.

Drinking helped me forget my problems... but that became the biggest problem of all.

On July 3rd, 2023, I had my last drink. I haven't touched alcohol since.

Getting sober forced me to face everything I had been running from. I burned bridges, damaged relationships, and spent a lot of years stuck in a cycle that felt impossible to escape. Slowly, with time and sobriety, I've started rebuilding those relationships and my life one day at a time.

Today I live in a sober house with other guys in recovery and it's one of the best environments I've ever been in. Being around people who are working on themselves has helped me stay focused and grateful for the life I have now.

I also deal with some difficult health issues. I have a neurological condition that causes my hands to shake constantly, and severe anxiety that sometimes makes it hard to even stand without feeling like I might fall. Because of this, working a normal job has been extremely challenging.

But sobriety has given me something I didn't have before: the motivation to create instead of escape.

I've always loved building things and working with technology. Recently I started designing and 3D printing a product called a Steam Friends Display — a small desk device that shows your friends list so you can see who's online while gaming. It's a simple idea, but people really like it and it's something I'm proud to build.

Right now production is slow because I only have limited equipment and materials. My goal is to grow this into a small business that allows me to support myself despite my health limitations.

Where your support goes
What the Funds Will Help With
  • Purchase additional 3D printing equipment
  • Buy materials (filament and parts) to keep up with orders
  • Improve manufacturing speed and quality
  • Help grow this into a sustainable small business

Every bit of help moves me closer to being able to support myself while continuing my recovery.

Sobriety has given me a second chance at life. Instead of running from problems, I'm trying to build something meaningful with the time I have now.

Thank you for reading my story and for supporting someone who is simply trying to move forward, one day at a time.

— Brian
